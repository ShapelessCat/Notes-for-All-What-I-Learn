1. The text contrasts the _pre-LLM era (2019)_ with today:
   1) programmers once had to search the web or wait on forums for answers,
   2) BUT now they can get instant, contextual help from LLM assistants—even have
      them write code interactively.
   This transformation is not limited to software engineering; it is reshaping
   nearly every field.

2. The driving force is that /LLMs/ are a revolutionary technology:
   they can perform almost any text-based task (generation, Q&A, data
   extraction, summarization, classification, translation) that formerly
   required human interaction, but at vastly higher speed and without fatigue.

3. For entrepreneurs, this opens immense opportunities. However, to seize them,
   one must be prepared. This book is a guide to understanding LLMs, mastering
   prompt engineering, and building valuable applications for users, companies,
   or yourself.

** DONE What You Will Learn - x
CLOSED: [2026-07-10 Fri 15:09]
- The goal of this book is to equip you with all the
  theory, techniques, tips, and tricks
  you need to master /prompt engineering/ and build successful LLM applications.

- Content:
  * Part I - we convey a foundational understanding of LLMs:
    1. their inner workings
    2. their functionality as text completion engines.

    We cover the extension of /LLMs/ to their new role as _chat engines_, and
    we present *a high-level approach* to LLM application development.

  * Part II - we introduce the _core techniques_ for /prompt engineering/:
    how to
    1. _source_ context information,
    2. _rank_ its importance for the task at hand,
    3. _pack_ the prompt (without overloading it), and
    4. _organize_ everything _into_ a template that will result in high-quality
       completions that elicit the answer you need.

  * Part III - we move to more advanced techniques:
    1. We _assemble_ /loops/, /pipelines/, and /workflows/ of LLM inference to
       create /conversational agency/ and /LLM-driven workflows/, and

    2. we then _explain_ techniques for evaluating LLMs.

- Throughout this book, we highlight one principle that underlies all others:
  *At their core, LLMs are just text completion engines that mimic the text they
  see during their training.*

  * If you process that statement deeply, then
    *you'll arrive at the same conclusions that _we share throughout this
    book_:*

    when you want an LLM to behave a certain way, you have to
    *SHAPE* the /prompt/ to _resemble patterns seen in training data_ --
    + use clear language,
    + rely upon existing patterns _RATHER THAN_ creating new ones, and
    + don't drown the LLM in superfluous content.

- Once you master /prompt engineering/, you can build upon these skills by
  _creating conversation agency and workflows_ -- the dominant paradigms for LLM
  applications.
