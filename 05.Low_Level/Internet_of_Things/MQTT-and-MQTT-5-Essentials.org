- /MQTT/ is a very lightweight and binary protocol, and due to its minimal
  packet overhead,

  * /MQTT/ EXCELS when transferring data *over the wire* in comparison to
    protocols like /HTTP/.

- The protocol is also extremely easy to implement on the client-side.

  Ease of use was a key concern in the development of /MQTT/ and makes it a
  perfect fit for *constrained devices with limited resources* today.

**** History Lesson
The MQTT protocol was invented in 1999 by _Andy Stanford- Clark (IBM)_ and
_Arlen Nipper (Arcom, now Cirrus Link)_. They needed a protocol for minimal
battery loss and minimal bandwidth to connect with oil pipelines via satellite.

- The two inventors specified several *REQUIREMENTS* for the future protocol:
  * Simple implementation
  * Quality of Service data delivery
  * Lightweight and bandwidth efficient
  * Data agnostic
  * Continuous session awareness (=from Jian= ???)
